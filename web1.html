Web 1.0

What It Is

Web 1.0 refers to the first era of the World Wide Web, roughly from 1991 to the early 2000s. It was a read-only web. Websites were mostly static HTML pages that delivered information but allowed no interaction. Users could read content but could not contribute, comment, or participate.

Characteristics

Pages were built by developers or webmasters and rarely updated. There was no user-generated content, no social features, and very little personalisation. Most sites were essentially digital brochures. Navigation was basic — links between pages, nothing more.

Technology

Web 1.0 sites were built with simple HTML, sometimes with frames, tables for layout, and animated GIFs. Server-side scripting was minimal. Databases were rarely involved. Pages were often hand-coded and uploaded via FTP.
