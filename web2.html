Web 2.0

What It Is

Web 2.0 is the second era of the web, from the mid-2000s to roughly the 2010s. The defining shift was from read-only to read-write. Users stopped being passive consumers and became active participants. They could post, comment, share, and create content.

Characteristics

Technology

Web 2.0 was powered by AJAX (which allowed pages to update without full reloads), CSS for richer design, JavaScript frameworks, and APIs that let different platforms share data. It also introduced the idea of 'the platform as a service' — companies provided infrastructure, users provided content.
