Extranet

What it is

An extranet extends a company's intranet to selected external users — typically partners, suppliers, or clients. It sits between a fully private intranet and the open internet. External users get limited, controlled access to specific parts of the internal network.

How It Differs from Intranet and Internet

An intranet is only for internal staff. The internet is open to everyone. An extranet is the middle ground — open to a defined group of outsiders who have been granted specific access. A good example is a supplier portal where vendors can log in to track orders and submit invoices without accessing the company's full internal systems.

Security

Because extranets involve outside users accessing internal systems, security is important. Access is usually protected by authentication, firewalls, and VPNs. Data shared through extranets is often encrypted.
