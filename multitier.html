Multitier Architecture

What It Is

Multitier architecture (also called n-tier architecture) is a way of structuring a software system by separating it into distinct layers, each with a specific role. The most common version is the threetier model: Presentation, Business Logic, and Data.

The three tiers

The Presentation tier is the front end — what the user sees and interacts with (the web page or app interface). The Business Logic tier is the middle layer — it processes user input, applies rules, and decides what to do with data. The Data tier is the back end — databases and storage systems where information is saved and retrieved.

Why it matters

Separating concerns into tiers makes systems easier to maintain and scale. You can update the front end without touching the database. You can change the business rules without rewriting the UI. Each tier can run on separate servers if needed, which improves performance and reliability.
