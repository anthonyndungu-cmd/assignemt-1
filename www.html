World Wide Web (WWW)

What it is

The World Wide Web is a system of interlinked documents and resources accessed through the internet. It was invented by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989 while he worked at CERN. People often use 'the internet' and 'the web' interchangeably, but they are different things. The internet is the physical network of cables and servers. How it works

Web pages are written in HTML and stored on servers. When you type a URL into your browser, the browser sends a request to the relevant server, which responds by sending back the HTML file. Your browser then renders that file as a visual page. Pages link to other pages through hyperlinks, which is what makes the whole thing a 'web.'

Why it matters

Before the Web, the internet existed but was mostly used by researchers and academics. The Web made it accessible to everyone by giving information a visual, clickable interface. It changed how people communicate, shop, learn, and work.
